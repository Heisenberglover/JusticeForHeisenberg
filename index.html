About Us

At Elite Horse Training, we believe every colt or horse deserves patience, fairness, and training that makes sense to them. We don’t force or rush — we give them the time they need to understand what’s being asked. Our approach is built around trust, respect, and keeping things clear.

Our Services

Groundwork Training

A calm horse starts on the ground. We spend plenty of time teaching colts how to lead, tie, stand quietly, and move off pressure. Groundwork is where respect and communication are built. When a horse understands the basics on the ground, everything else becomes easier.

Colt Starting

Getting a colt started the right way makes all the difference. We start slow — saddling, handling, and introducing new experiences in small steps. Our focus is on keeping colts relaxed and curious, never scared. A good start means a horse that’s confident for life.

Behavior Fixes

Some horses pick up bad habits — biting, kicking, refusing to load, pulling away. Instead of punishment, we figure out why they’re doing it. Then we work with patience and repetition until the bad habit is replaced with better behavior. Every horse can learn to relax and do better.

How We Train

Training colts and older horses isn’t about force — it’s about building steps that make sense. Here’s how we approach it:

Step 1: Trust First

We start with simple handling — brushing, picking up feet, leading calmly. Once a colt trusts us on the ground, we can ask for more without stress.

Step 2: Clear Communication

Horses learn by release and pressure. We keep cues clear and reward the right try. This keeps the colt thinking and willing instead of confused or defensive.

Step 3: Small Wins

Training is broken down into small steps. A colt might learn to accept a saddle pad one day, then a light saddle the next. Each win builds confidence.

Step 4: Consistency

Horses remember routine. By keeping things steady, colts quickly figure out what’s expected and relax into the work.
